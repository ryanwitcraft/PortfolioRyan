Geovisualization Projects by Ryan Witcraft

Sheep Per Horse Per State (1850)

This map uses two colors and a gradient between them to distinguish the ratios of sheep per horse in each state. The darker the color means the higher ratio of sheep per horse.

Washington D.C. Crime per Voting Precinct (2024)

This is a proportional symbol map that displays the quantity of crimes committed per precinct in Washington D.C. The bigger the circle, the more crimes that were committed in that precinct.

Amount of Crimes per Voting Precinct vs Percent Committed During Midnight Shift in Washington D.C. (2024)

This is a multivariate choropleth map displaying amount of crimes in Washington D.C. The more red, the more happened during the midnight shift. The more blue, the more crimes.

Impaired Lakes and Streams in Iowa (2020)

This is a basic symbol map that shows the impaired lakes and streams within Iowa borders. This displays part of the geometric data that was used in order to conduct the Final Project

Impaired Streams in Iowa (2020)

This is a symbol map that displays how many impairments each river has within Iowa. These impairments range from 1-5 and have different severity levels that are further delved into in the Final Project notebook.

U.S. Migration from 1887-1924

This is a flow map that shows migration patterns in the U.S. The colors and thickness of the flows display the amount of people moving, the size of the circles display how many people are moving into the state, and the color of the state shows how many people are moving out.
